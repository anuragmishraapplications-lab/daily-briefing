Indonesia Issues Ministerial Regulation Introducing Age Restrictions for Social Media Platforms

Indonesia's government issued Ministerial Regulation No. 9 of 2026 on March 12, establishing a risk-based framework for child protection across social media platforms. The regulation sets out criteria for determining the risk level to children from contact with unknown persons, exposure to violent or inappropriate content, exploitation as consumers, and threats to children's personal data security. The move follows Australia's December 2025 legislation requiring platforms to block users under 16, and positions Indonesia as part of a growing global wave of child-safety-focused digital governance reforms. The regulation operates under the earlier Government Regulation No. 17 of 2025 on Governance of Electronic System Operation for Child Protection.
