Supreme Court Strikes Down IEEPA Tariffs; White House Pivots to Section 122 and Section 301

The U.S. Supreme Court ruled that tariffs imposed under the International Emergency Economic Powers Act exceeded statutory authority, immediately lowering rates on goods from China, India, and Brazil. In response, President Trump signed an executive order imposing a 10% tariff on all countries under Section 122 of the Trade Act of 1974 -- a rarely invoked mechanism limited to 150 days without congressional approval. Separately, USTR Jamieson Greer launched Section 301 investigations into 16 economies, potentially paving the way to reimpose the invalidated tariffs through new legal channels. The trade landscape remains highly volatile as all three mechanisms play out simultaneously.
