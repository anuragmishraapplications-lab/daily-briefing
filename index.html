IEA Agrees to Largest-Ever Emergency Oil Release — 400 Million Barrels — Amid Iran War Supply Crisis

The Wall Street Journal first reported on March 11 that the International Energy Agency had secured agreement from all 32 member nations for the largest emergency oil-stockpile release in its 51-year history: 400 million barrels, more than double the 182 million barrels released after Russia's invasion of Ukraine in 2022. The action came as the U.S.-Israeli war on Iran has reduced tanker traffic through the Strait of Hormuz to less than 10% of pre-conflict levels, creating what the IEA called "the biggest oil supply disruption in history." Iran threatened that "not one litre of oil" would pass the strait. The U.S. will contribute 172 million barrels from the Strategic Petroleum Reserve. Despite the announcement oil prices held above $90 per barrel, as markets doubted the release could offset 8 million barrels per day in lost supply.
