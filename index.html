White House Unveils National AI Legislative Framework With Seven Policy Pillars

The Trump administration released its National Policy Framework for Artificial Intelligence on March 20, laying out seven legislative recommendations for Congress. The framework calls for federal preemption of conflicting state AI laws, regulatory sandboxes to spur innovation, child safety measures on AI platforms, and free speech protections against government-driven censorship. Notably, it recommends against creating any new federal AI rulemaking body, instead favouring sector-specific oversight through existing regulators. While non-binding, the document sets the administration's negotiating position ahead of what could become the first comprehensive federal AI legislation.
