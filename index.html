OECD Anti-Corruption Outlook 2026: Countries Reform Laws but Struggle with Implementation

The OECD released its second Anti-Corruption and Integrity Outlook on March 24, covering 62 countries — the first time the report has achieved truly global scope. The core finding is a troubling implementation gap: across OECD members, the average strength of integrity regulations stands at 63%, but actual implementation rates lag at just 44% — a 19 percentage-point gap. In partner countries the gap widens to 26 points. The report assesses national integrity systems across seven areas including lobbying transparency, political finance, conflict-of-interest rules, and the independence of disciplinary systems for civil servants, and calls for governments to invest more in results-oriented reforms backed by data and new technologies.
